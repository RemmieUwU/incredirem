Hi! I'm Rem, though some people also call me Rin or Blueberry. I'm a 20-year-old Japanese girl with an endless curiosity for learning new things. My journey began when I was just 5 years old. I remember sitting at my dad's computer, pressing random keys on the keyboard, trying to figure out how everything worked. While other kids were busy playing video games, I was diving into the world of coding, animations, and trying to create things from scratch. I always had big dreams of becoming a programmer.

In 2015, I started making my first games, though, to be honest, most of them weren't very good. But things took a turn in 2018 when I moved to Poland. There, I pushed myself even harder, learning more every day. I teamed up with a friend to create some great games, but despite our hard work, nobody really played them. It was a tough blow, and for a while, it took away my drive to make games.
In the midst of it all, I decided to return to a childhood favorite of mine: Incredibox. I'd been a part of the game's community for years, and when the Dystopia update dropped in 2020, I saw an opportunity. This time, I came back with new skills, unique content, and a passion for coding. To my surprise, people loved it! I was shocked by the attention and support I received it was something I never expected, but it reignited my motivation.

Since then, I've been creating MODs for Incredibox, and some of them have become pretty popular! You might have heard of "Evadare," "Xrun," "Void," or "The Bells." Each one is a reflection of the joy I found in creating something special for a game and community that means so much to me.
